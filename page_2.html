Inventorium London

Building Tomorrow's Innovators Today

A unique STEM lab for kids focused on robotics, programming, and teamwork.

My Story: From Parent to Founder

As a family with 15 years in IT, we wanted to build something meaningful for our children and community. After exploring STEM programs in Ukraine and Canada, we saw gaps in structure, motivation, and hands-on outcomes — leading to the creation of Inventorium London.

The Problem: Current STEM Education Gaps

  • Limited hands-on engagement beyond block coding tools.
  • Lack of tangible results and long-term motivation.
  • Few opportunities for teamwork and AI literacy.

Market Data & Opportunity

Ontario’s EQAO (2023–24) shows stagnant math scores. PISA data reveals gaps in student proficiency. Meanwhile, STEM enrollment and demand for technical skills continue to rise, underscoring the need for early intervention programs like Inventorium.

Competitive Landscape

London offers options like Code Ninjas and Maker Labs, but they lack structure, team projects, and public showcases. Inventorium bridges that gap with 12-week team-based robotics challenges ending in Demo Days.

Our Solution: Inventorium Program

  • 12-week project semesters (3-hour weekly sessions)
  • Teams of 5 with rotating roles
  • AI integration and ethics module
  • Public Demo Day and Robot Battles

Learning Outcomes & Unique Value

  • Teamwork and leadership through collaborative projects
  • Project delivery discipline (planning, sprints, testing)
  • Logic and algorithmic thinking via Python and control systems
  • Integrated approach combining hardware, software, and creativity

Business Model & Financials

  • 12-week semester: $699 (early-bird $649)
  • Break-even at 9–10 students
  • Projected Year-1 ROI: 2.5–2.9x
  • Sibling discount (10%) and need-based scholarships

Progress & Next Steps

  • Establish founding cohort waitlist and pilot program
  • Partner with schools, PTAs, and makerspaces
  • Launch first cohort and public Demo Day
  • Target: $21K–$24K net income in Year 1

The Ask: Partnership

  • Mentorship on program and pricing strategy
  • Space partnership for pilot cohorts
  • Grant navigation and micro-funding
  • Corporate introductions for Demo Day

Partnering will help us create an accessible, modern STEM pipeline for London’s youth — fostering innovation and community impact.
